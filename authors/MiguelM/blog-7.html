Managing Communications

26 March, 2022

Introduction

Communication is probably the most important factor in success or failure of any interpersonal relationship. Therefore, since society is composed of people who can’t read each other’s minds, it follows that communication also plays an essential role in the success and failure of relationships at all other levels as well. This is especially true when it comes to Communication in the context of Project Management. The Merriam-Webster Dictionary defines communication as “a process by which information is exchanged between individuals through a common system of symbols, signs, or behavior” (Merriam-Webster). Good communication, therefore, can be defined as the effective and accurate exchange of information between two entities. Within the realm of Project Management, the responsibility to manage communications lies not only with the Project Manager, but also with every member of the team, to ensure that all information conveyed helps to further the goals the team has set for themselves beforehand.

Technology in Communication

When utilized correctly, technology may make the process of generating and disseminating knowledge easier. E-mail, instant messaging, websites, telephones, cell phones, texting, and other technologies are used by the majority of individuals and organizations to communicate. You may develop and organize project documentation, timetables, meeting minutes, and client requests in an electronic format using a project management information system. This data can be saved locally or on the cloud. Electronically storing templates and examples of project papers can make standard forms more accessible, making information exchange easier. Backup measures should also be in place in the event that typical communications methods fail. The communication technique and medium are two crucial factors to consider when determining which technologies to utilize.

Types of Communication

Technologies are not the only factors that affect the delivery of communication; there are also different types of communication which help the message be delivered in the best possible way in order to convey the original message. There are three main types of communication: interactive communication, push communication, and pull communication. An expert defines interactive communication as “simultaneous and continually occurring messages that account for the manner in which previous messages react to other messages” (IGI Global). The word "interactive communication" is self-explanatory. It is a multidirectional kind of communication, as the name implies. When you need a quick response and the information you're sharing is sensitive and might be misunderstood, interactive communication is the way to go. It entails one or more individuals sharing ideas and thoughts in real time, with participants responding in kind. Another type of communication is push communication. This communication type is usually understood as “communication that is delivered by the sender to the recipients” (PMLS). This type of communication goes from sender to recipient, and it's best when you're disseminating information but don't need a quick response—or if the information you're attempting to transmit isn't urgent or sensitive. However, as soon as the recipient sees the message, he or she must take action. Memos, letters, emails, reports, faxes, and other forms of push communication are examples. It is usually done through a textual medium rather than face-to-face. One drawback of this kind of communication is that the sender can only tell if the message was delivered properly. Although some email systems can tell you when an email has been opened, this function does not guarantee that the receiver has comprehended the content. The last type of communication is pull communication. Pull communication is usually defined as “a communication method that provides access to the information however the receiver must proactively retrieve the information” (PMLS). Pull communication does not include enlisting the help of a reader to read your message. Pull communication, on the other hand, is all about giving a group of people access to the same information. The receiver, on the other hand, must retrieve this data. This strategy is employed when there is a huge group of people that need information. Use this type of material when your conversation is informational. It will have no impact on the project if a recipient does not read your communication. Pull communication is regarded to be the most suitable type of communication since information will only be accessible when the need arises. Posting information on websites, wikis, knowledge repositories, bulletin boards, and other places is one example of pull communication.

Performance Reporting Tools

Performance reporting is another crucial technique for managing project communications. Stakeholders are kept informed about how resources are being used to meet project goals through performance reporting. It also encourages employees to report on their success. Progress reports are regarded as a fantastic tool—one that is widely utilized and proven to boost project performance. Progress or status reports are common formats for performance reporting. Many people confuse the two names, although some people note significant differences between them. A progress report is a “document that shows the progress that your team is making towards completing a project” (ScrumGenius). This report is essentially a project management method for preventing problems before they arise, ensuring that the project is completed on schedule, and keeping all stakeholders informed about the project's progress. The frequency with which the progress report should be submitted (daily, weekly, monthly, etc.) will be determined by the extent and complexity of the project. The other tool for effectively reporting performance is status reports. These can be defined as “a communication tool that project managers use to keep tabs on their employees’ work experiences” (ScrumGenius). A weekly status report can be done in person, but it's much easier to conduct it online. The report will be used by team members/employees to inform their supervisor about their emotional or project-based development for the current and next weeks. As a project manager or team lead, a weekly status report is one of the most effective methods to communicate with your team. The report will be used by a good team leader to assess a team's weekly progress on projects or emotional well-being. Weekly status updates allow you to have shorter meetings and increase workplace openness. The reports also make it possible for teams to collaborate in a seamless manner.
